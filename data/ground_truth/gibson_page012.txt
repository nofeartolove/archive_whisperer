January. SUNDAY,10. 1864. 
Plymouth N.C. This morning cold 
but pleaasant. first thing in order 
is Sunday Morning Inspection to 
day the Rev. Mr. Rowley the future 
Chaplain of the 103rd P.V. was introduced 
to sa. regt. to day.  I am detailed 
for duty. Corp of Provost Guard. there 
is nothing new to record. Mail goes out today.
MONDAY, 11.
Plymouth N.C. This morning cold and 
ground hard frozen. but morning pleasant. 
relieved from Provost guard at 
9 A.M. feel rathe unwell to day. This 
P.M. there was a review of the 
Veterns of the 103rd 101st & 85th N.Y. Inf. 
there was a "Right Smart" show of 
Strength. Still I could only be a spectator.
TUESDAY, 12.
Plymouth N.C This morning 
cold damp and chilly, there is 
nothing new to record to day. 
Morning went to Picket line 
to Market. To day we draw our 
outfit of clothing for Jan 1864 
the examination of men for
the vteran volunteer service 
is in progress to day.
WEDNESDAY, 13. 1864.
Plymouth N. C. This day damp and 
disagreeable; Spend most of the day 
in writing, making out a new requisition 
for clothing for Co.B. 103rd 
also; in making out the enlisting 
papers of the Vetern volunteers of 
Co.B. 103rd 36 in number. to day Co. 
B's Photograph was taken, but not good.
THURSDAY, 14.
Plymouth N.C. This morning 
rainy & disagreeable. Glad that 
I am not on duty although 
this day was my turn for duty. 
but I am exempt being considered 
upon daily duty settling 
up the old accts of Co B. 103rd P.V. 
This the night of singing attendance sm.all.
FRIDAY, 15.
Plymouth N.C. this morning more 
cheerful & the sun shines out bright 
& warm for the first time in many 
days. I am very busy to day making 
out invoices of ordnance & Ordnance 
stores & receipts of clothing, camp & 
Garrison equipage. All is quiet to 
day. this night again the night 
for singing.
