June, WEDNESDAY, 8. 1864.
Military Prison Andersonville Ga! This morn-|ing Gloomy & Foggy. My individual condition |is bad enough but not to be compared to |the condition of many men in this camp. |I have got the diarrhœa checked, and to day |I feel tolerably well. The day is intensely hot. |I spend part of the time studying Algebra, |a few more Prisoners brought in. No news. |

THURSDAY, 9.
Still in this Hell-upon-earth of a Prison |our condition is daily growing more disa-|greeable as the weather grows warmer; three out |of four of my mess, are sick and I would be sick |too, but it won't do. Reader, I will here give a |list of some of the ruling prices of this place; ried |per bw. $26.00 U.S. currency, wheat flour $64.00 per bw. |salt, $128.00, Eggs $4.00 per dz, Molasses $12.00 per gall. |P.M. rain. |

FRIDAY, 10.
Military Prison Andersonville Georgia, |"When'er I take my walk abroad; |How many poor I see; |What shall I render to my God, |For all his gifts to me; |Not more than others I deserve, |Yet God has given me more," |For. I have health while others lie, |And pine away and die.
No changes to day.

June, SATURDAY, 11. 1864.
There is nothing new to record to day, the |same dull monotony of Prison life has to |be enacted over again. There is a world of |suffering in this camp. Soldiers naked |without protection from the almost vertical |rays of the sun; wholesome food & med-|icines our condition could hardly be worse. |Write a letter to my Wife to day. |

SUNDAY, 12.
To day is the holy sabbath; but there is |no sabbath here; O Liberty, Law & Order |Thou canst not be appreciated till thou |art once lost. Here are 20,000 men coop'd up |in about 12 acres of space; surrounded by a |high Stockade; mounted by sentinels & commanded |by Artillery; the Heavens of brass overhead, No signs |of vegetation far or near; God hasten Deliverance. |

MONDAY, 13.
This morning most disagreeable; cold rain |every day in the so called Confederacy but |makes our condition worse; bying on the |wet ground in our wet clothes; & one half the |men already sick; & you can scarcely im-|agine a worse case. The rations issued to |the inmates of this Prison consists of a very |limited portion of boiled rice & a little meal |The day continues cold wet & disagreeable. |feel considerably ill, but it is no use to complain.
